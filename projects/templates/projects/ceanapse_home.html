Our Story

The Genesis and Resilience Amidst Adversity

The history of Ceanapse is broad, extensive, timely, and vibrant, demonstrating true resilience among its members. Our journey began in 2005 when our founder, as a young child in Homabay town, observed the continuous threats posed to the Lake Victoria shorelines by climate change and anthropogenic activities.

This vision for community impact was further solidified during the 2007 post-election violence in Kenya. During this period of unprecedented physical movement restrictions, fish became the main source of food, which led to adverse implications for women involved in fishing activities‚Äîincluding small-scale fishing, fish trading, and value addition. It was during this time of uncertainty and fear that the foundational stones for establishing our blue economy-oriented Non-Governmental Organization were laid.

Today, Ceanapse is recognized as one of the newly established international NGOs, with our main office located in Nairobi, Kenya, serving as a beacon of hope and transformation for coastal communities across the region.
